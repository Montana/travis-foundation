Travis Foundation managed and supported Rails Girls Summer of Code as their first community program and we've helped run and manage the initiative over the summer.

The program

Rails Girls Summer of Code funds women around the world to work full-time over summer on open-source software projects. Modelled after Google Summer of Code, but with a focus on beginners and women wanting to get into Open Source programming. It is the first ever event of its kind in the world. It began 1st of July, 2013, and ran for three months.

The teams

19 sponsored and 10 volunteering students from all over the world participated in the program and worked on Open Source projects like Sinatra, Rails, Rubinius, Bundler, Hackety Hack, Diaspora, Species+, impress.js and more!
